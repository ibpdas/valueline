Methodology - How to read this dashboard

Model explanation, flow and notes.

How the model works

This dashboard uses a three level causal model showing how Level 1 foundational enablers influence Level 2 maturity and Level 3 outcomes. It is not a benchmark. Performance data is illustrative and does not represent any real organisation.

Understanding the three levels

Level 1 foundations - Conditions that data leaders and teams shape and control. These are co-designed and contextual. This example includes strategy alignment, shared leadership, common foundations, grassroots community and cross-functional engagement.

Level 2 maturity - Rrepresents how data is used in practice across uses, data, leadership, culture, tools and skills. These are six themes from Government Data Maturity Framework (2023). It emerges from Level 1 foundations and evolves more slowly.

Level 3 outcomes - Shows public value i.e., savings, satisfaction, efficiency, time saved and system contributions. Public value is created when maturity is in place. They are lagging indicators and take longest to materialise.

Indicator types

  • Leading indicators provide early signals of enabling conditions at Level 1.
  • Signal indicators reflect developing system behaviours and adoption at Level 2.
  • Lagging indicators reflect realised benefits and outcomes at Level 3.

Methodology and evidence sources

  • Delivery artefacts, trackers and governance papers inform Level 1.
  • Annual maturity assessments, surveys and engagement insight inform Level 2.
  • Verified benefit cases, reuse metrics and public value indicators inform Level 3.

Caveats and intended use

This model simplifies complex systems and is intended for strategic discussion and learning, not audit or assurance. Outcomes lag behind foundations and maturity. Desired outcomes should inform future foundation design.
