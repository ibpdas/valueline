How the model works
Three levels that connect foundations, maturity and public value.
This dashboard uses a three level causal model that shows how Level 1 foundational enablers influence Level 2 maturity and Level 3 outcomes and impact. It is not a benchmark and the performance data is illustrative only.
METHOD
Understanding the three levels
What each level represents in practice.
Level 1 foundations LEADING

Conditions that data leaders and teams shape and control. In this example they cover strategy alignment, shared leadership, common foundations, community and engagement.

Level 2 maturity SIGNAL

How data is used in practice across uses, data, leadership, culture, tools and skills. These follow the Government Data Maturity Framework (2023) and evolve more slowly over time.

Level 3 outcomes and impact LAGGING

Organisational outcomes and longer term public value such as savings, satisfaction, efficiency, time saved and wider public contributions. These indicators lag behind foundations and maturity.

LEVELS
Level 2 maturity detail
Six dimensions using the Government Data Maturity Framework.
Uses, Data, Leadership, Culture, Tools and Skills describe how well the organisation turns data into outcomes and impact. Ratings run from 1 Beginning to 5 Mastering and reflect the maturity that emerges from Level 1 foundations.
Theme Level Notes

DETAIL
Indicator types and signals
How to read the badges and status signals.
LEADING Early signals about inputs, foundational work and delivery progress.
SIGNAL Emerging behaviours and adoption patterns across the system.
LAGGING Realised outcomes and impact that move later in time.
  • Leading indicators sit closest to Level 1 and show progress on foundations, inputs and visible deliverables.
  • Signal indicators sit around Level 2 and describe how people, processes and tools are changing.
  • Lagging indicators sit at Level 3 and show the realised outcomes and impact from earlier work.
INDICATORS
Methodology and evidence sources
Where each level draws its evidence from.
  • Level 1 foundations use delivery artefacts, project trackers, governance papers and risk logs.
  • Level 2 maturity uses annual assessments, staff surveys, user research and engagement insight.
  • Level 3 outcomes and impact use verified benefit cases, reuse metrics, performance indicators and public value measures.
EVIDENCE
Caveats, responsible use and attribution
How to interpret this dashboard responsibly.

This model simplifies complex systems and is intended for structured strategic discussion, learning and planning. It is not designed for audit, formal assurance or performance rating.

Outcomes and impact will always lag behind foundations and maturity. Desired outcomes and impact should inform future foundation design, and the dashboard should be read alongside detailed evidence and professional judgement.

This model builds on concepts originally presented by Bandhu P. Das at DAMA UK and Big Data LDN 2023. Watch the talk here.
