How the model works
Three levels that connect foundations, maturity and public value.

The model uses a simple causal chain: Level 1 foundations â†’ Level 2 maturity â†’ Level 3 outcomes and impact. It helps leaders interpret progress in the right order and avoid unrealistic expectations of outcomes before system conditions are ready.

This three-level structure helps set realistic expectations: Level 1 moves fastest, Level 2 takes longer, and Level 3 lags behind both.
METHOD
Understanding the three levels
What each level represents in practice.
Level 1 foundations LEADING

Strategy, leadership, foundations, community and engagement work that create the earliest signals of progress.

Level 2 maturity SIGNAL

System behaviour across uses, data, leadership, culture, tools and skills. Changes appear gradually as conditions improve.

Level 3 outcomes & impact LAGGING

Measurable value such as savings, satisfaction and time saved. These move last and require aligned maturity.

LEVELS
Indicator types and what they tell you
A shared language for interpreting progress.
  • Leading indicators â€“ early signs that foundational work is taking effect.
  • Signal indicators â€“ how the system is responding and adopting new practices.
  • Lagging indicators â€“ realised outcomes and public value.

Different indicators move at different speeds â€” this prevents unrealistic pressure for outcomes before conditions are ready.

INDICATORS
Mixed-method measurement
Why multiple evidence types are needed.

The model encourages a balanced approach, combining quantitative, qualitative and economic evidence. No single metric captures capability or public value â€” mixed methods provide a fuller picture.

  • performance reports
  • leadership updates
  • cost & time savings

EVIDENCE
Caveats and responsible use
Interpret this dashboard with care.

The methodology simplifies complex systems and supports strategic discussion. It is not a benchmark, audit tool or assurance mechanism.

It is not a data management or governance framework (e.g., DAMA or DCAM) and does not replace organisational governance, risk controls or formal valuation models.

Bias reminders

Use these prompts when interpreting diagnostics and selecting ValueLine shifts.

Cb

Cobra effect

Risk

Incentives create unwanted behaviour.

How it appears

Focus on outputs such as dashboards or datasets instead of real value.

Mitigation

Use outcome indicators and feedback loops to ensure value is created.

Gh

Goodhart's law

Risk

Measures become targets and lose meaning.

How it appears

Maturity scores are inflated or dashboards are designed to stay green.

Mitigation

Use shift indicators and test what the metric actually reveals.

Av

Availability bias

Risk

Recent or memorable issues dominate judgement.

How it appears

Recent crises or loud anecdotes are treated as enterprise truth.

Mitigation

Look across all teams to find consistent patterns and signals.

Pr

Present bias

Risk

Short term wins over long term value.

How it appears

Shiny use cases are prioritised over data quality or standards.

Mitigation

Show trade offs and connect foundations to later speed.

Cf

Confirmation bias

Risk

Only looking for evidence that matches existing views.

How it appears

Data quality issues are rejected or AI is seen as the answer before diagnosis.

Mitigation

Use challenge questions and search for evidence that contradicts assumptions.

Fp

False precision

Risk

Believing complex systems can be quantified exactly.

How it appears

Over confident scoring or narrow maturity levels without evidence.

Mitigation

Use ranges, confidence levels and uncertainty notes.

Sv

Survivorship bias

Risk

Learning from winners but ignoring the long tail.

How it appears

High performers are copied without understanding system constraints.

Mitigation

Compare all teams and look for structural barriers.

Op

Optimism bias

Risk

Underestimating cost, time and complexity.

How it appears

Ambitious timelines without enough resource or sequencing.

Mitigation

Use pre-mortems and delivery risk checks.

Sc

Sunk cost fallacy

Risk

Past investment drives future decisions.

How it appears

Legacy platforms or failing projects are kept alive because of prior spend.

Mitigation

Use stop criteria and review options independently of history.

Hl

Halo effect

Risk

Overgeneralising from strong teams or leaders.

How it appears

Champions are used to represent system maturity.

Mitigation

Look for system wide indicators and variability.

Cx

Complexity blindness

Risk

Ignoring dependencies across culture, skills, governance and platforms.

How it appears

Shifts are treated as isolated actions.

Mitigation

Use causal links and read across to understand enablers.

Ln

Linear thinking

Risk

Expecting smooth, predictable progress.

How it appears

Culture or governance maturity is assumed to move step by step.

Mitigation

Plan for dips, pauses and non linear change.
